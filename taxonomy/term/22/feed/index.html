field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><p><span style="font-size:16px">Setting up micro-farms for ex-offenders in Greater Manchester, this presents the outcomes of applying permaculture principles to an innovative project growing legitimate instead of criminal crops.   </span></p> <p><span style="font-size:16px">Launched in 2015, the project recycles the farming skills learnt on illegal crops by providing recovering ex-offenders with a shipping container or mobile home urban 'farm'. As part of a supportive co-operative, the 'refarmers' aid their own recovery and personal resilience by becoming self-employed.</span></p> <p><span style="font-size:16px">Greater Manchester is the UK’s cannabis growing capital with 1,000 plants seized a week and Salford has the most seizures in Greater Manchester. Those 8 tons of cannabis come with mountains of hydroponic equipment that takes police time to record, dismantle, transport and dispose of in landfill sites against a backdrop of public sector cuts.</span></p> <p><span style="font-size:16px">Refarming dramatically cuts the energy and fossil fertiliser inputs associated with hydroponic technique with digital sensors and renewables, aiming to make the farms 'off-grid'.</span></p> <p><span style="font-size:16px">While non-soil based growing systems (used for over 80% of UK vegetables) cannot be certified organic, Refarming takes a look at how the design of 'industrial' farms can still build in permaculture principles.</span></p> <p><span style="font-size:16px">It invites comment on how 'ponic' and 'natural' farming systems can co-exist rather than represent moral opposites.</span></p> <p><span style="font-size:16px">Instead, dismantled cannabis farms seized in raids by police will be redistributed to ex-offender ‘Refarmers’ with hydroponic growing experience working in a multi-stakeholder ‘Reforming Cooperative’ comprising  the growers themselves, local community criminal justice and business support agencies and Refarming end-users (customers) who together will form a strong, commercially sustainable recovery community.</span></p> <p><span style="font-size:16px">These outputs will be achieved through the location of one ‘Training Refarming Box’ at either HMP Styal or HMP Thorn Cross and then up to five operational boxes in community locations for cooperative members to run when they are released.
