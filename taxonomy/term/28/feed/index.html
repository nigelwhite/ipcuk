The aim is to understand more about the range of small holdings, what and how much they are producing, and to create a data set which can be compared with the productivity of larger farms. Case study in Iceland - Growing food at 66ºN https://www.ipcuk.events/presentation/Arctic-Permaculture-Case-study-Iceland <div class="field field-name-title field-type-ds field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><h2>CANCELLED: Arctic Permaculture. Case study in Iceland - Growing food at 66ºN</h2></div></div></div><div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><p><span style="font-size:16px">We present a case study of how food can be grown in a polar type of climate. In the far away reaches of the Arctic Circle, where northern lights dance, volcanoes stop airplanes, and people believe in elves, there is an ecocommunity that thrives in the middle of Iceland, Sólheimar (“the house of the sun”).</span></p> <p><span style="font-size:16px">For the last 4 years, I experimented with everything that concerns growing food in such a cold climate. Besides geothermal heated greenhouses, I focused in growing food outdoors, using techniques to deter the challenges of the harsh Icelandic climate.</span></p> <p><span style="font-size:16px">Raised beds facing south were lifted with local tundra mulch, with windbreaks around. Perennial edibles planted (siberian pea, silverberry, lupins, good king henry, walking onions, groundnut or rhubarb). Tomatoes of siberian varieties were cropped outdoors, to the disbelief of locals. We even tried corn, quinoa or perennial peppers. Self-sowing kale greens became like a weed, eaten year-round as dried kale chips. Herbs were grown used for teas and handcrafted cosmetics. We planted berries, perennial grains and fruit trees. Exciting possibilities exist: hazelnuts, very hardy citrus or hardy kiwi. Our collected knowledge is of immense value for those at the northern edges of US, Canada, Scandinavia, Scotland or Russia.</span></p> <p><span style="font-size:16px">If one can establish permaculture here, where snow/frost is possible anywhere in the year, summer rarely above 15ºC and winds blow at hurricane strength, then we can apply permaculture anywhere in the planet.
