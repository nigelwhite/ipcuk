Contribute

Designing the World We Want: the 12th International Permaculture Conference

We have invited contributions from academics, sustainability activists, permaculture practitioners, artists, engineers, smart city planners, thinkers and dreamers, organisations and networks, and others to help shape and deliver the two day programme.

Contributions will address one of the two major themes of the conference.

The first is the future that has already arrived: What are the inspiring, far-sighted projects of today, that show us how a future sustainable society will look?

The second is the pathways to transformation: How do we get from where we are now to where we want to be?

 

Together we'll create a vision of a near future society that is caring, sustainable and fair, and explore how we can collectively design pathways to make it happen. The vision will be based on cutting edge research and exemplary practice, building on what we know is possible, today.

 

Conference Aims

  • Explore and share how existing tools and processes can be used to design the world we want and need.
  • Enhance cooperation and links between different groups and sectors working to achieve a more sustainable world.

And whilst everyone is there, we will launch the Permaculture International Research Network (PIRN) too!

 

Participants

We are taking a transdisciplinary approach and invite participation from: permaculture practitioners; transition (towns) and other community-based sustainability and climate change groups; eco-villagers; researchers and academics; international & UK NGOs; UK & EU policy makers, agencies & government departments; students; businesses; professional bodies; artists, animators & video makers. We are also inviting a number of 'keynote listeners'.

 

Structure and Scope

Each day will include plenary sessions in the 1000-seat main conference room, plus break-out sessions in seven additional rooms. Within the overall theme of 'Designing the World We Want', each day has a focus:

  • Day One: PRACTICE. Case studies from existing exemplary projects and reports on the latest research evidence, helping describe what a sustainable society could look like in 2030.
  • Day Two: PROCESS.

 

Call for Academic and Practitioner Contributions - deadline now passed

People from a great variety of disciplines and topics are already working towards the theme of 'Designing the World We Want' - will you represent your area in this discussion? We see this conference as an opportunity to further our collective understanding of how permaculture and other integrated ecological approaches are being used to design sustainable and resilient systems at household, community, regional, national and international scales.

Practitioner contributions are important to this; we are hoping that there will be roughly equal volumes of academic and non-academic content.
 

Whilst we recognise that contributions will cut across disciplines we will group them into the following broad strands:

  •     Built Environment
  •     Tools and Technology
  •     Education and Culture
  •     Health and Wellbeing
  •     Finance and Economics
  •     Land Tenure and Community Governance
  •     Land and Nature Stewardship

 

Deadlines

We will assess submissions on a rolling basis as they come in, and shape the programme around them, so we encourage early applications. The final deadline for submissions is Friday 27 March 2015. Decisions on workshop invitations will be circulated by Friday 24 April.

We will make some programme details available in mid-May 2015.

A final programme for the conference will be available from Friday 31 July 2015.

 

Conference languages

The working language of the conference will be English. If you would like to submit a session in another language, please contact us to discuss details.

 

Finances

The only current source of income for IPCUK is registration fees, which have been set on a break-even basis. If we attract further funding, it may be possible to offer subsidised places for people on low incomes and lacking access to institutional support. We welcome offers to collaborate on fundraising, and encourage session organisers and other participants to be proactive in seeking institutional and/or third party support.
