Convergence Workshops

Below, a selection of the convergence workshops are detailed. This is to give a flavour of what's on offer.

A document of all workshop descriptions can be downloaded as a PDF.

Design Your Polyincome with the Polyincome Slider

Bob Mehew

 

Bob has developed a permaculture design process and tools for polyincome based on the OBREDIMET design framework. It focusses on the relationship between income, outgoings and how people choose to spend their time, evaluating the trade-offs between them in a visual, interactive way.

Activity type:

Practicals, Presentation

Theme:

Diploma

Professional Hand Drawing

Sophie Ellison

 

This workshop is for people who want to gain skills in representing their design on paper with professional drawings. Sophie will go through initial sketching techniques, before showing participants drawing techniques specific to three phases of permaculture design; site analysis, conceptual design and detailed drawings.

Activity type:

Practicals

Theme:

Diploma

Biochar

Albert Bates

 

Come along to learn how, when and why to make & apply biochar; develop cascading yields of probiotic feed supplements, water cleaning, restoration of degraded soils, blackwater to energy systems, invasives to energy systems, carbon-negative ecovillage, hotel and business design, simple kiln design & microenterprise potentials.

Activity type:

Discussion, Practicals, Presentation

Theme:

Ecological Practice

Drought Proofing Strategies - your foundation to Abundance

Jeremiah Kidd

 

A presentation on how to create an oasis in the high desert by honoring, conserving and reusing water in multiple ways.

 

Participants will come away with real world examples of regenerative water relationships and drought proofing your designs in a changing climate.

Activity type:

Presentation

Theme:

Ecological Practice

Flush Free

Marc Van Hummelen

 

A Q&A on composting toilets. Many still have doubts about the compost toilet: which system to choose, safety, how to use the compost. Marc can answer all those questions from his own experience and scientific research on the subject. After a short intro on compost toilets and Marc's favorite design, Jenkins' loveable loo, participants can fire away.

Activity type:

Discussion, Presentation

Theme:

Ecological Practice

Goats in Permaculture

Beck Lowe

 

Goats can be a useful provider of milk, meat and weed control (amongst other things), but they can also be very destructive and capricious. This workshop will discuss how to design a goat into a permaculture system, their yields and needs, the time commitments involved, and other aspects of goat keeping.

Activity type:

Presentation

Theme:

Ecological Practice

Introduction to Woolfelt

Liesbeth Bos

 

Learn the process of felting from a raw fleece, and understand how it works.

 

By the end of the session we will have created a rug from raw wool!

Make Your Own Medicine - elderberries are ace!

Anna Broszkiewicz

 

Come along to discover the magic of elderberries. This will be a demonstration, knowledge sharing and tasting session of making elderberry syrups including:
* Picking, preparing, cooking and bottling
* Why bother to make this and other herbal medicines? - Ideas and Inspiration
* Inviting participants to share their knowledge and recipes

Activity type:

No-Dig Gardening - why and how

Aljaz Plankl

 

This workshop will explain what no dig gardening is, the benefits, and how to go about it, including setting up and maintaining your garden. Aljaz will present a number of pictures which demonstrate the rseults of no-dig, as well as exploring soil life basics and no-dig techniques. Participants should come away with the courage and knowledge to try no-dig gardening for themselves.
